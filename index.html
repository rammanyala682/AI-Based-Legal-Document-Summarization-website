Problem Statement

Legal professionals often spend significant time reading and analyzing lengthy legal documents, case laws, contracts, and court decisions. These documents are complex, full of technical jargon, and contain crucial information scattered throughout. This project aims to develop an AI-based solution to automatically summarize long legal documents, focusing on the most important legal information.

Project Objectives

  1. Develop an AI-based Summarization System: Build a machine learning model that can condense long legal documents into concise summaries while preserving key information, such as legal arguments, clauses, and decisions.
  2. Leverage Advanced NLP Techniques: Utilize transformer-based models like BERT or GPT to enhance the model's ability to understand and summarize complex legal text. Implement text summarization techniques to generate human-readable summaries.
  3. Enhance Information Extraction: Incorporate Named Entity Recognition (NER) to identify key legal entities, such as parties, legal statutes, and dates, to enrich the summaries and provide contextual relevance.

Analysis Overview

Dataset Analysis: The dataset comprises Indian Supreme Court judgments, structured to support abstractive summarization tasks. These documents are rich in legal jargon and structured hierarchically.

Model Analysis: Use transformer models such as BERT, GPT, or other legal-specific NLP models to generate summaries.

Performance Evaluation: Metrics such as ROUGE and BLEU are used to evaluate the model, alongside human expert validation.

Key Features

  • Summarization Techniques:
    • Extractive Methods: Models like SummaRuNNer extract key sentences based on importance.
    • Abstractive Methods: Models like BART and Pegasus generate summaries that rephrase or reorganize information.
  • Datasets: IN-Abs, IN-Ext, UK-Abs datasets for legal summarization tasks.
  • Models Implemented: BART, Legal-LED, Pegasus for abstractive summarization; SummaRuNNer for extractive summarization.
  • Preprocessing Tools: Advanced tokenization and sentence embedding techniques.
